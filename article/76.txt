Some time after 10,000 BC, people made the first real attempt to control the world they lived in, through agriculture. Over thousands of years, they began to depend less on what could be hunted or gathered from the wild, and more on animals they had raised and crops they had sown.

Farming produced more food per person than hunting and gathering, so people were able to raise more children. And, as more children were born, more food was needed. Agriculture gave people their first experience of the power of technology to change lives.

By about 6000 BC, people had discovered the best crops to grow and animals to raise. Later, they learned to work with the seasons, planting at the right time and, in dry areas, making use of annual floods to irrigate (灌溉) their fields.

This style of farming lasted for quite a long time. Then, with the rise of science, changes began. New methods meant that fewer people worked in farming. In the last century or so, these changes have accelerated. New power machinery and artificial fertilizers (化肥) have now totally transformed a way of life that started in the Stone Age.
